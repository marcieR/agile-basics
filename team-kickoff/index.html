Getting Started

Team Kickoff & Working Agreements

Before your first sprint, your team needs one sprint to get aligned. Here's how to run it.

🚀

Sprint 0

Not a planning sprint — a setup sprint. The time your team spends aligning on how you'll work before you commit to what you'll build.

👥 Who: Scrum Master + full team
📅 When: Before Sprint 1
⏱️ Duration: 1–2 days
🏗️ Framework: Scrum
Purpose

Sprint 0 is not a planning sprint. It's a setup sprint — the time your team spends aligning on how you'll work before you commit to what you'll build. Skip it and you'll spend the first 3 sprints discovering misalignments that could have been resolved in a day.

What Sprint 0 IS:

Establishing working agreements, building your DoD, configuring tools, running practice ceremonies, defining team norms.

What Sprint 0 is NOT:

Delivering features, padding the backlog, writing every story for the next quarter.

💡 Keep It Focused

Keep it short. One day for a co-located team, two days for distributed. The goal is alignment, not exhaustion. If you're still in Sprint 0 after a week, you've lost the plot.

📋

A Two-Day Agenda

A practical schedule for getting your team aligned and ready to ship — before Sprint 1 begins.

Day 1 — Alignment
  • 1
    Team introductions & working styles (30 min)

    How does each person prefer to communicate? What's their working hours? How do they like to receive feedback?

  • 2
    Roles & responsibilities (45 min)

    Walk through each role's responsibilities. Use the roles page as a reference. Confirm who is playing what role.

  • 3
    Draft working agreements (60 min)

    See the working agreements section below. Get the first version on paper.

  • 4
    Tool setup & access (60 min)

    Jira, Slack, GitHub, Confluence — make sure everyone has access and knows the conventions.

  • 5
    Definition of Ready workshop (45 min)

    Build your DoR as a team. What makes a story ready to start?

Day 2 — Process
  • 1
    Definition of Done workshop (60 min)

    Build your DoD as a team. What makes a story done?

  • 2
    Practice refinement (60 min)

    Run a real refinement session with backlog items. Use it to stress-test your DoR.

  • 3
    Sprint 1 planning (90 min)

    Plan your first real sprint. Commit to a sprint goal.

  • 4
    Retrospective on Sprint 0 (30 min)

    What worked in the setup process? What do you want to change before Sprint 1?

  • 5
    Celebrate 🎉

    You have a team. That matters.

🤝

Working Agreements

The rules your team sets for itself. Not imposed from above — negotiated together.

Working agreements are the explicit norms that prevent friction later. The conversations that feel awkward to have in Sprint 3 ("why does no one review PRs?") are easy to have in Sprint 0 when everything is hypothetical. Surface them early.
Topics to Cover
  • ✓Core hours: when is everyone expected to be online/reachable?
  • ✓Response time: how quickly should Slack/email messages be answered?
  • ✓Code review: what's the turnaround SLA for PR reviews?
  • ✓Meeting norms: cameras on/off? Agenda required? How do we handle being late?
  • ✓Decision making: how do we make technical decisions? Who has final say?
  • ✓Definition of blocked: what counts as a blocker? When do you raise it?
  • ✓Feedback style: how direct is direct? How do we give feedback in retros?
  • ✓Out of office: how much notice for planned absence? Who covers?
Rules for the Agreements Themselves
  • 1

    Everyone must agree — not just nod. If someone has reservations, surface them now.

  • 2

    Write them down and post them somewhere visible — team wiki, Confluence, or the first page of your Jira project.

  • 3

    Revisit in every retrospective — working agreements rot. Update them when reality changes.

  • 4

    Violations get named, not ignored — "hey, that's not what we agreed" is a healthy conversation. Letting it slide erodes the agreements faster than the original violation.

⚡

Making Sprint 1 Work

Your first sprint sets the norms for everything that follows.

Five Tips for Sprint 1
  • Undercommit deliberately. Sprint 1 velocity is unknown. Take 70% of what you think you can do. Finishing early is a better outcome than the first sprint ending in carry-over.
  • Run every ceremony, even if it feels over-engineered. The team needs reps. Don't skip standup because "we're all in the same room." Muscle memory comes from repetition.
  • Treat the retrospective as the most important ceremony. Sprint 1 will surface things that Sprint 0 didn't anticipate. The retro is where you fix them before they compound.
  • Name your sprint goal out loud. At standup, reference it. At review, measure against it. The sprint goal is the team's shared commitment — not just a label on a board.
  • Don't change the working agreements mid-sprint. Wait for the retro. Changes during a sprint undermine the agreements before they've had a chance to establish.
💡 Remember

The most important thing about Sprint 1 is finishing it — not what you finished. A team that ships something and holds a retrospective is ahead of a team that over-committed and carried half their backlog over.

Sprint 1 Outcome A working team with a shared history, a retro with real feedback, and a velocity baseline for Sprint 2 planning.
